📐 How the Calculation Works
1

Tokenization & Preprocessing

Each document is lowercased, stripped of punctuation, split into tokens, and filtered through a stop-word list. Only meaningful content words remain.

tokens = [w for w in words if w not in STOP_WORDS and len(w) > 1]
2

Term Frequency (TF)

For each document, we calculate the normalised frequency of every term — how often it appears relative to the total token count.

TF(t, d) = count(t in d) / |d|
3

Inverse Document Frequency (IDF)

IDF down-weights terms that appear in many documents (common words) and up-weights rare, distinctive terms. We use the smooth variant from scikit-learn.

IDF(t) = log( (1 + N) / (1 + df(t)) ) + 1
4

TF-IDF Vectors

Each document becomes a high-dimensional numeric vector where each dimension corresponds to a vocabulary term and its value is TF × IDF. This is the document's "fingerprint" in vector space.

TF-IDF(t, d) = TF(t, d) × IDF(t)
5

Cosine Similarity

We measure the cosine of the angle θ between the two TF-IDF vectors. A value of 1 (θ = 0°) means identical, 0 (θ = 90°) means no overlap whatsoever.

cos(θ) = (A · B) / (||A|| × ||B||)    ∈ [0, 1]
6

Similarity Percentage

The final similarity percentage is simply the cosine value multiplied by 100. The cosine angle θ is derived via arccos and shown in degrees for geometric intuition.

Similarity% = cos(θ) × 100    θ = arccos(cos(θ))
